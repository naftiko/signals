Mostly the things that interest us are best described as outputs of these systems, since the output determines most of the input. - Designing Freedom, Stafford Beer

We want to stitch together a "Fabric" of insights in these areas:

Companies - Gathering signals from 30+ companies across 15 separate industries to understand the current state of AI integration.
Investments - Evaluating the signals of the investments made by companies that will shape how AI is integrated into the enterprise.
Services - Understanding what the top services in use across a company, shaping what resources teams have to integrated into AI.
Domains - Organizing resources available across services into logical business domains that reflect what we are trying accomplish.
Maturity - Evaluting the value generation as well as the producationa and commoditization of services we are depending upon.
Platform - Tracking on where a company is in its platform journey, shaping the resources teams can be leveraging for AI integration.
Pricing - Consider the pricing across cloud, SaaS, and AI integrations to understand the cost of change and evolution present.

Allow you to walk away with insights into the following areas:

Cost - Having an understanding of the pricing models and the cost of using the services you depend on to integrate with AI.
Velocity - Understanding what you are capable of so you can find the right velocity for teams operating within each domain.
Risk - Possessing a clear understanding of the risk involved across the services you depend upon to do the business you need.
Innovation - Embracing your legacy so that you can meet the future by managing cost, velocity, and risk to allow you to innovate.

Then go back to your regular workday:

Thinking - This is an interesting view of landscape, and it provides me with more awareness against the competition.
Feeling - That you are either ahead or behind, either way you have more awareness that will result in more confidence
Doing - Following up with Naftiko, sharing more about what you have learned, and going back to do more research.
